Dear Mr Boomer

Further to our letter of 27 November the Commission has now made its assessment of your complaint under the Code.

The Commission members have asked me to thank you for giving them the opportunity to consider the points you raise. However, their decision was that there was no breach of the Code. They took this decision after having reviewed your comments and they have asked me to let you know their reasons for this in more detail.

The Commission noted your genuine concerns about both the tone and substance of the article. It noted that you did not believe that the thrust of the article was accurate, but considered that the newspaper had substantiated its claims and its involvement with the story, including relevant quotations. The Commission therefore considered that no grounds for inaccuracy, and therefore no breach of Clause 1 (Accuracy) of the Code, had been established. The Commission then turned to the alleged breach of Clause 3 (Privacy). However, it noted that the newspaper had not appeared to have included anything that had not been placed in the public domain by previous coverage, and considered that the article did not represent an intrusion into the life of the victimâ€˜s family in breach of Clause 3 of the Code. Whilst the Commission offered its deepest sympathies to you and your family, and acknowledged that you were still in a period of grief, it did not consider that the article had been intrusive towards, or sought to intrude into, this grieving process. Consequently, the Commiss
ion concluded that no breach of Clause 5 (Intrusion into grief or shock) had been established in this instance.

Although he Commissioners have taken this decision, they have asked me to send a copy of your letter to the editor so that he is aware of your concerns.

Thank you for taking this matter up with us.

Yours sincerely
